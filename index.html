FAQ

Quick answers before you book.

How do I book a session?

Start with one of the two options in Quick-Start: a free plan or a $100 intro session. After you submit the intake form, you‚Äôll pick a time on Calendly.

Why can't I book other services directly?

During launch, I start everyone with the same process to ensure quality and consistency. Once we've connected through Quick-Start, I can tailor everything from there - artist passes, mobile recording, custom projects, all of it.

What's the difference between a Recording Session and Band Capture?

Recording session prioritizes precision, isolation, and performance detail. Band rehearsal capture prioritizes comfort + speed so you can review arrangements and share demos.

What do I leave with after the session?

Every session includes organized exports/stems plus a rough mix. Add-ons like final mixes and photo packages are available, or included with Artist Passes.

What's required to confirm a time?

A $50 deposit is required to confirm your session time and is applied to the final total. Full details are in the Deposit & Cancellation Policy.

How long should I book?

There's a 2-hour minimum on hourly sessions. For band rehearsal capture, booking a longer block is recommended so setup doesn't eat your creative time.

What should I bring?

Bring your session files (stems, instrumentals, or project sessions), references, and anything unique to your sound. If you're tracking vocals, bring water and any preferred headphones.

When should I book off-peak vs standard?

Off-peak is best when you want a quieter building (especially for delicate vocals). Review the Noise policy so I can recommend the best window.

What if I‚Äôve never recorded before?

Most clients haven‚Äôt. That‚Äôs exactly what the Launch Intro is for - I‚Äôll guide every step, from setup to playback. No experience needed.

Can I reschedule my session?

Yes. With 72+ hours notice, your deposit is fully refundable or transferable. Between 24-72 hours, it converts to studio credit valid for 60 days. Full details in the cancellation policy.

What if I‚Äôm not happy with the recordings?

We‚Äôll listen back together before you leave. You‚Äôll know exactly what you‚Äôre taking home - and if something needs another pass, we‚Äôll talk through the best next step.

Recording Session vs. Band Capture

Recording Session

Precision-focused. Track vocals and instruments one at a time (or in small groups) with isolation and detailed engineering. Ideal for polished takes, layered production, and getting the best performance on each part.

Band Rehearsal Capture

Comfort-focused. The whole band plays together and I capture everything live through an 8-input interface with 5 headphone stations. Ideal for reviewing arrangements, making demos, or documenting the energy of your rehearsal.

Which should I pick?

If you want release-quality recordings with careful attention to each part, go with a Recording Session. If you want to capture what your band sounds like in the room - quickly and without overthinking - Band Capture is the move.
